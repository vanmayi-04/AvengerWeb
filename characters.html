The Avengers

Iron Man

A billionaire genius and founding Avenger who built the Iron Man suit to save the world.

Captain America

Super-soldier and leader of the Avengers; known for his strength, shield, and values.

Thor

The Norse God of Thunder from Asgard, wields the enchanted hammer Mjölnir and later Stormbreaker.

Hulk (Bruce Banner)

A brilliant scientist who transforms into a powerful green giant when angry, later becomes “Smart Hulk” by combining brains and brawn.

Black Widow (Natasha Romanoff)

A skilled spy and assassin, once a Russian operative who becomes a core Avenger with a strong moral compass.

Hawkeye (Clint Barton / Kate Bishop)

Master archer and S.H.I.E.L.D. agent, family man, and original Avenger.

Scarlet Witch (Wanda Maximoff)

Wields chaos magic and reality-warping powers. Her grief over Vision leads to major multiversal consequences.

Vision (J.A.R.V.I.S. / White Vision)

An android created from Vibranium, the Mind Stone, and Stark's AI J.A.R.V.I.S. Later reprogrammed as White Vision after his original death.

Falcon (Sam Wilson)

Former Air Force pararescueman with mechanical wings. Becomes the new Captain America.

War Machine (James “Rhodey” Rhodes)

Tony Stark’s best friend, uses a heavily armed version of the Iron Man suit. Loyal and battle-hardened.

Ant-Man (Scott Lang / Hank Pym)

A thief-turned-hero who uses a suit that shrinks or enlarges him.

Deadpool

A wisecracking mercenary with accelerated healing, fourth-wall-breaking humor, and a knack for chaos. Not your typical hero, but undeniably unforgettable.

Wasp (Hope van Dyne / Janet van Dyne)

Hank’s daughter, expert combatant who shrinks and flies.

Spider-Man (Peter Parker)

A teen with spider powers and a genius mind. Mentored by Iron Man; known for his quick wit and strong morals.

Doctor Strange (Stephen Strange)

Former neurosurgeon turned Sorcerer Supreme. Master of mystic arts, protects reality from magical and multiversal threats.

Black Panther (T’Challa / Shuri)

Noble king of Wakanda, enhanced by the heart-shaped herb.

Captain Marvel (Carol Danvers / Monica Rambeau)

Carol Danvers: Cosmic superhero with energy powers and flight after exposure to the Tesseract. Monica Rambeau: Gains energy manipulation abilities after passing through Wanda’s Hex.

Winter Soldier (Bucky Barnes)

Steve Rogers’ best friend, brainwashed into a Hydra assassin. Later redeemed and fights for good.

Star-Lord (Peter Quill)

Leader of the Guardians of the Galaxy, half-human and half-Celestial, known for his charm and love of music.

Gamora

Adopted daughter of Thanos, deadly assassin with a conscience, member of the Guardians.

Drax the Destroyer

Warrior who seeks revenge for his family’s death; literal-minded but fiercely loyal to the Guardians.

Rocket Raccoon

Genetically modified raccoon and tech genius, with a sharp tongue and soft heart.

Groot

A tree-like being who can regenerate and grow; limited speech (“I am Groot”) but emotionally expressive.

Mantis

Empathic alien who can sense and influence emotions; innocent and kind-hearted.

Nebula

Adopted daughter of Thanos and Gamora’s sister, turns from a rival to a loyal Guardian and Avenger.

Shang-Chi

Martial arts master raised in isolation by his warlord father. Wields the mystical Ten Rings.

Ms. Marvel (Kamala Khan)

Teen fangirl of superheroes, especially Captain Marvel. Gains light-construct powers from a mystical bangle.

She-Hulk (Jennifer Walters)

Bruce Banner’s cousin, a lawyer who gains Hulk-like powers after a blood transfusion. Balances law and superhero life.

Moon Knight (Marc Spector / Steven Grant / Jake Lockley)

Man with dissociative identity disorder who becomes the avatar of Egyptian god Khonshu. Each identity has unique traits.

Echo (Maya Lopez)

Deaf martial artist with photographic reflexes. Once Kingpin’s enforcer, later seeks her own path.

America Chavez

Teen hero with the ability to punch star-shaped portals between dimensions; vital to multiverse travel.

Ironheart (Riri Williams)

Teen engineering prodigy who builds her own Iron Man-style suit; inspired by Tony Stark’s legacy.

Main Villains

Thanos

The Mad Titan who seeks to wipe out half the universe using the Infinity Stones.

Loki

The God of Mischief and Thor's adopted brother; flips between villain and anti-hero.

Ultron

An AI created by Tony Stark who turns against humanity, seeking to cause extinction.

Hela

The Goddess of Death and Thor’s sister who seeks to conquer Asgard.

Killmonger

A revolutionary who challenges T’Challa for the throne of Wakanda with radical plans.

Red Skull

A Nazi officer and head of Hydra; cursed to guard the Soul Stone on Vormir.

Alexander Pierce

A high-ranking S.H.I.E.L.D. official and secret Hydra operative in Winter Soldier.

Agatha Harkness

A powerful witch hiding in Westview, manipulating events behind the scenes in WandaVision.

Kingpin

Crime lord of New York with vast power and influence, appearing in Daredevil and Hawkeye.

Kang the Conqueror

A multiverse-traveling villain who manipulates time and reality with dangerous variants.

Green Goblin

Norman Osborn, a genius driven mad who becomes Spider-Man’s most dangerous enemy.

Mysterio

Illusionist and former Stark employee who fakes heroism to manipulate the world.

Taskmaster

Mind-controlled assassin with the ability to mimic any fighting style instantly.

The High Evolutionary

A cruel scientist who experiments on living beings to create a "perfect" society.

Gorr the God Butcher

A grieving father turned god-slayer after being betrayed by his deity.

Namor

Ruler of Talokan who clashes with Wakanda to protect his underwater kingdom.

Supporting Roles

Pepper Potts

CEO of Stark Industries and Tony Stark's partner; dons the Rescue armor in Endgame.

Happy Hogan

Stark Industries’ head of security and close friend of Tony Stark; often helps Spider-Man.

Maria Hill

A high-ranking S.H.I.E.L.D. agent who works closely with Nick Fury in global security ops.

Nick Fury

Former S.H.I.E.L.D. director who formed the Avengers Initiative; protector of Earth from cosmic threats.

Darcy Lewis

Astrophysicist who works with Jane Foster and later investigates Westview anomalies in WandaVision.

Jane Foster

Astrophysicist and Thor’s love interest who later wields Mjolnir and becomes the Mighty Thor.

Wong

The current Sorcerer Supreme who safeguards magical knowledge and assists Doctor Strange.

Korg

A friendly Kronan warrior who fights alongside Thor and offers comic relief in battle.

Ned Leeds

Peter Parker’s loyal and humorous best friend who helps him navigate superhero life.

MJ

Peter Parker’s clever and grounded girlfriend; a unique reinterpretation of Mary Jane.

Yelena Belova

Natasha Romanoff’s sister and a Black Widow agent with wit, skill, and deadly talent.

Agent Coulson

S.H.I.E.L.D. agent who died in Avengers and was later revived in the Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. series.

Sif

A fierce Asgardian warrior and ally of Thor who fights to protect the realm.

Heimdall

Guardian of the Bifröst with the ability to see and hear across realms; loyal to Thor.

Talos

A shape-shifting Skrull general who becomes an ally of Nick Fury and Captain Marvel.

Mobius M. Mobius

An agent of the TVA who works with Loki to stop time anomalies and uncover TVA secrets.

Sylvie

A female Loki variant determined to bring down the TVA and uncover her true destiny.

Miss Minutes

An animated TVA mascot who plays a surprising role in maintaining the timeline.

Kamran

Kamala Khan’s friend who gains powers linked to the Noor dimension in Ms. Marvel.

Layla El-Faouly

Marc Spector’s wife and a brave archaeologist who becomes the Scarlet Scarab in Moon Knight.
